<p><p>CAR-T therapy, a form of immunotherapy for hematological malignancies, involves modifying a patient's T cells to target cancer cells. In the autologous market, T cells are harvested from the patient, genetically engineered, and re-infused. Conversely, the allogeneic market utilizes T cells from a healthy donor, offering a more immediate treatment option. Autologous CAR-T provides personalized treatment, while allogeneic approaches promise scalability and accessibility, allowing for broader patient populations to receive effective therapies against blood cancers.</p></p>
