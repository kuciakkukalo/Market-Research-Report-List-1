<p><p>Novel Drug Delivery Systems (NDDS) enhance therapeutic efficacy and reduce side effects. Liposomes encapsulate drugs in lipid bilayers, improving stability and targeting. PEGylated proteins and polypeptides extend circulation time and reduce immunogenicity. Polymer nanoparticles offer controlled release and targeted delivery, while protein-drug conjugates combine proteins with therapeutics for precise action. Other NDDS types include micelles, dendrimers, and viral vectors, each designed to optimize drug absorption and distribution, ultimately improving patient outcomes across various medical conditions.</p></p>
