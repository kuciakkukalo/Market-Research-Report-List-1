<p><p>Lighting control occupancy sensors help optimize energy use by detecting human presence. The market includes several types: </p><p>1. **PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors** detect body heat and movement, making them effective in smaller spaces.</p><p>2. **Ultrasonic Sensors** emit sound waves and detect motion by analyzing the returned frequencies, suitable for large or complex areas.</p><p>3. **Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors** combine PIR and ultrasonic technologies for increased accuracy and reliability.</p><p>4. **Others** may include specialized sensors designed for unique applications or environments.</p></p>
