<p><ul><li>High Voltage Grid</li><li>Extra-High Voltage (EHV) Grid</li><li>Ultrahigh Voltage (UHV) Grid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Super Grid market types include High Voltage Grid, Extra-High Voltage (EHV) Grid, and Ultrahigh Voltage (UHV) Grid, each defined by transmission voltage levels. High Voltage Grids operate typically between 100 kV and 220 kV, primarily serving regional electricity distribution. EHV Grids, ranging from 220 kV to 800 kV, facilitate long-distance power transfer with minimal losses. UHV Grids exceed 800 kV, enabling the transmission of vast amounts of electricity over extensive distances, essential for intercontinental energy sharing and renewable integration.</p></p>
