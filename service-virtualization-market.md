<p><p>Service Virtualization is a technique that simulates the behavior of dependent services or components in a software application, enabling developers and testers to work independently of these services. It allows testing and development to occur in a controlled environment, reducing delays and improving efficiency by providing access to services that may be unavailable, costly, or time-consuming to use directly.</p><p>The Service Virtualization Market is experiencing significant growth driven by the increasing demand for agile development practices and continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD) methods. As organizations focus on enhancing their software quality and accelerating time-to-market, the adoption of service virtualization is becoming more prevalent across various industries, including finance, healthcare, and retail. This growth is further supported by the escalating need to integrate complex enterprise systems and manage microservices architectures.</p><p>Recent trends indicate a shift toward cloud-based service virtualization solutions, enabling scalability and flexibility for development teams. Additionally, the integration of advanced technologies such as AI and machine learning is enhancing the capabilities of service virtualization tools, making them more intuitive and effective. The Service Virtualization Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.3% during the forecast period.</p></p>
